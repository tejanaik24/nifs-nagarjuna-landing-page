WELCOME TO!

FIRE AND INDUSTRIAL SAFETY
DEPARTMENT

The Department of Fire and Industrial Safety was established in 18th September, 2025 by the esteemed personalities Sri. Dr. Gangadhara Rao Kancharla, I/c Vice Chancellor, Smt. Prof. K. Ratna Shiela Mani garu, Rector [I/C], Sri Prof. G. Simhachalam garu, Registrar [I/C], Sri Suneel Mahanty, C.E.O. and Chairman and offering Bachelor Degrees in Fire and Industrial Safety on self-supporting mode. The department inaugurated two regular and on-campus courses i.e., Bachelor of Science (B.Sc.) Honors in Fire and Industrial Safety Four years and Bachelor of Science (B.Sc.) in Fire and Industrial Safety three years for the academic year 2025-2026.

Sri. SUNEEL MAHANTY

M.Sc., M.Phil., MBA(FISM)

C.E.O. and Chairman, NIFS

ACHIEVEMENTS & EVENTS

Achievements

Short Term Training Program (STT)-Ergonomic Safety Steward The Department of Fire and Industrial Safety, ANU was acted as one of the nodal agencies to organize Short Term Training Program (STT) - Ergonomic Safety Steward program under the Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana (PMKVY) with the coordination of Andhra Pradesh Productivity Council (APPC), Government of Andhra Pradesh to enhance skill development among the youth of University and Affiliated Colleges of ANU.

ACHIEVEMENTS & EVENTS

Events

The 55th National Safety Day/Safety Month- March 4th, 2026 As per the guidelines of National Safety Council (NSC), India, the Department of Fire and Industrial Safety, ANU was successfully organized the 55th National Safety Day/Safety Month on March 4th, 2026 under the theme "Engage, Educate and Empower People to Enhance Safety. The event highlighted the active participation of Students, Teachers and Public for fostering a proactive safety culture in work place.

Events

Fire Services Week (FSW) - 14–20 April, 2026 The Department of Fire and Safety, ANU, has been organized Fire Services Week (FSW) in remembrance of the tragic fire and explosions at Victoria Dock, Mumbai Port, on 14th April 1944. This week is dedicated to raising awareness and strengthening fire prevention measures across industries, workplaces, schools and offices.
